Common Mugwort

Common Mugwort (Artemisia vulgaris) is an herbaceous plant often found in nitrogen-rich soils. The plant's scent is reportedly a mild insect repellant. Common Mugwort is used as an oferring in Nepalese temples. Tradition holds that it has cleansing properties, so believers also use it to sweep their floors to give them both a spiritiual and physical cleaning.

Tips from Garden Coaches

Common Mugwort is cultivated as an ornamental and aromatic herb out of the 300 species of Artemisia genus. With its toughness and repellents, common mugwort is a good schoice for you to protect your garden from nasty pests with an intricate border.
